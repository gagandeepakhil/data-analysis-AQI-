Exploring Air Quality Determining Factors in Visakhapatnam Using Data Analysis¶

This is a course project as part of my zerotopandas course from jovian.ml.The course is really awesome and made me understood what really data analysis and how it can be used to solve real life problems...

Introduction

This project aims to analyze and explore the air quality determining factors in Visakhapatnam over the course of nearly one year. The dataset used in this analysis contains various parameters such as From Date, PM2.5, PM10, NO, NO2, NOx, NH3, SO2, CO, Ozone, Benzene, Toluene, Temperature (Temp), Relative Humidity (RH), Wind Speed (WS), Wind Direction (WD), Solar Radiation (SR), Barometric Pressure (BP), Virtual Wind Speed (VWS), Ambient Temperature (AT), Rainfall (RF), Xylene, and Total Rainfall (TOT-RF).

Dataset Source

The dataset for this project was obtained from https://cpcb.nic.in/. It is crucial to provide proper attribution to the dataset source to ensure transparency and acknowledge the efforts of data collectors and providers.

Project Objectives

The primary objectives of this project are to gain insights into the air quality determining factors in Visakhapatnam and their potential impact on the environment and public health. By analyzing the dataset, my specific objectives include:

Temporal Analysis: Explore the temporal patterns and trends of various air quality parameters over the course of nearly one year. By examining the data at different time intervals (daily, monthly, seasonal), we aim to identify patterns of variation and potential seasonality in air quality factors.

Pollutant Assessment: Evaluate the levels of pollutants such as PM2.5, PM10, NO2, SO2, CO, and others, and assess their compliance with regulatory standards or guidelines. This analysis will provide an overview of the air pollution levels in Visakhapatnam and help identify periods of elevated pollution.

Correlation Analysis: Investigate the relationships and correlations among different air quality factors. By examining the interdependencies between variables such as temperature, humidity, wind speed, and pollutant concentrations, we aim to identify potential contributing factors to air pollution.

Data Visualization: Utilize Python libraries like Pandas, Matplotlib, NumPy, and Seaborn to generate informative and visually appealing plots and charts. Through data visualization techniques, we aim to effectively communicate the findings of the analysis and highlight significant trends or relationships.

By accomplishing these objectives, we aim to contribute to the understanding of air quality in Visakhapatnam, facilitate evidence-based decision-making, and support initiatives for improving environmental conditions and public health.

Data Preparation and Cleaning¶
Data preparation and cleaning are crucial steps in any data analysis project, ensuring that the dataset is in a suitable format for analysis and free from inconsistencies or errors. In this project, we have followed a systematic approach to prepare and clean the dataset containing air quality determining factors in Visakhapatnam. The following steps were undertaken:


Dataset Loading:


The dataset was loaded into a Pandas DataFrame, a powerful data manipulation tool in Python, allowing easy handling and analysis of the data.


Dataset Structure Examination:


An initial exploration of the dataset was conducted using Pandas functions such as head(), info(), and describe(). This step provided insights into the column names, data types, and summary statistics of the variables.


Handling Missing Values:


Missing values were identified by using the isnull() function to check for null entries in each column. Based on the nature and amount of missing data, appropriate strategies were applied:


Rows with relatively small and randomly distributed missing values were dropped using the dropna() function.
Missing values in significant columns were imputed using suitable techniques like mean, median, mode imputation, or more advanced methods such as regression imputation or machine learning algorithms.


Handling Duplicate Data:


Duplicate entries were detected using the duplicated() function, and any duplicates found were removed using the drop_duplicates() function.


Ensuring Consistent Formatting:


Consistency in data formatting was ensured throughout the dataset. Date and time variables were appropriately formatted, capitalization was standardized, and any inconsistencies in categorical variables were addressed.

#Before going into further analysis ,let us calculate the aqi for each month
#we define breakpoints as per india guidelines for calculation naqi
breakpoints = {
    'PM10': [0, 50, 100, 250, 350, 430],
    'PM2.5': [0, 30, 60, 90, 120, 250],
    'NO2': [0, 25, 50, 100, 200, 400],
    'SO2': [0, 40, 80, 380, 800, 1600],
    'CO': [0, 1, 2, 10, 17, 34],
    'Ozone': [0, 50, 100, 168, 208, 748],
    'NH3': [0, 100, 200, 400, 800, 1200],
    'RH': [0, 40, 60, 80, 100]
}
#let us define a function for calculating sub-index value of each substance
import numpy as np

def calculate_aqi(concentration, breakpoints):
    for i in range(len(breakpoints) - 1):
        if concentration >= breakpoints[i] and concentration <= breakpoints[i+1]:
            aqi = ((breakpoints[i+1] - breakpoints[i]) / (breakpoints[-1] - breakpoints[0])) * 100
            aqi = np.round(aqi, decimals=0)
            return aqi
    return None
#let us create a new column called AQI in the dataframe and then calculate them using max function in numpy...
df_grouped['AQI'] = np.nan

for index, row in df_grouped.iterrows():
    aqi_values = []
    for pollutant in breakpoints.keys():
        concentration = row[pollutant]
        aqi = calculate_aqi(concentration, breakpoints[pollutant])
        aqi_values.append(aqi)
    df_grouped.loc[index, 'AQI'] = np.nanmax(aqi_values)

Inferences and Conclusion¶
After analyzing the given data and performing various exploratory analyses, we can draw several inferences and conclusions:


    

  • Monthly Variation of Pollutant Levels: The bar plot showing the monthly average pollutant levels provides insights into the variations of different pollutants over time. We observed that PM10 levels were consistently high throughout the months, while other pollutants such as NO2, SO2, CO, and PM2.5 showed some variations but generally remained within acceptable limits.
    

    • 

  • Correlation Between Pollutants: The scatter plot matrix helped us visualize the relationships between different pollutants. We observed positive correlations between PM10 and PM2.5, indicating that higher levels of PM10 were associated with higher levels of PM2.5. Additionally, we observed positive correlations between PM10 and CO, and between PM2.5 and CO, suggesting a potential relationship between these pollutants.
    

    • 

  • Monthly Variation of AQI: The line plot of the AQI over time showed the monthly variations in air quality. We observed that the AQI remained relatively stable with occasional spikes in certain months, indicating fluctuations in air quality. The highest AQI values were recorded during the months of January, indicating potentially poor air quality during that period.
    

    • 

  • Impact of Pollutants on AQI: The correlation analysis between AQI and individual pollutants revealed that PM10, PM2.5, and CO had the strongest positive correlations with AQI. This suggests that these pollutants have a significant impact on air quality, and their levels are closely related to the overall AQI.
    

    • 

  • Relationship Between AQI and Relative Humidity: The scatter plot and correlation coefficient analysis between AQI and RH indicated a weak negative correlation. This suggests that higher relative humidity levels were associated with slightly lower AQI values. However, the correlation was not very strong, indicating that other factors may also influence air quality.
    

    •

For example, PM10 and PM2.5 levels were consistently higher during certain months, possibly due to weather conditions, emissions, or other factors specific to those months. In conclusion, the analysis of the given air quality dataset provided valuable insights into the variations in pollutant levels, their correlations, and their impact on the overall AQI.
